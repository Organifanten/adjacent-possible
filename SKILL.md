Ideation through the adjacent possible: what's one step from what exists. Each iteration maps the **frontier** — the edge between the actual and what could be — and advances along it. Every discovery reshapes the frontier.

## 1. Map the actual

Lay out what exists right now. Use the `research` skill to ground claims in primary sources when the actual is uncertain. An **element** is the smallest concrete unit in the landscape — whatever counts as a meaningful building block: a technology, a concept, a constraint, a capability. Name the elements, their properties, and the relationships between them. Don't speculate — the map is a ground truth that the rest of the process builds on.

**Done when:** every entry is named, has concrete properties, and no element is vague or hypothetical.

## 2. Map the adjacent possible

List what's one step from the actual. Adjacent means composable from existing elements, filling a gap in the current landscape, or recombining what's already there. One step — not two, not speculative.

**Done when:** multiple concrete possibilities are listed, each traceable to the existing elements, and every entry is genuinely adjacent rather than aspirational.

## 3. Advance the frontier

Pick one adjacent possibility to realise. Explore it concretely — name its properties, its implications, what it would take. Then remap the actual: does the realised thing change what exists? New elements create new adjacencies that weren't there before.

**Done when:** the possibility has been explored with concrete properties, and the actual is updated to reflect what's changed.

## 4. Iterate or stop

Return to step 2 with the updated landscape. Repeat the loop — map what's adjacent, advance, remap — until one of two natural stopping points:

- The frontier has thinned: few viable adjacent possibilities remain, and no new adjacencies are forming.
- A specific idea crystallised: one possibility emerged clearly through the iterations as the thing to pursue.

Stop and present what emerged.
